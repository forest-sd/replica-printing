Large Format Printing Guide

Banners, signage, displays, and more — what you need to know.

What Is Large Format Printing?

Large format printing refers to any print output wider than 18 inches — typically ranging from 24" to 60" wide and virtually unlimited in length. It's used for banners, posters, trade show displays, signage, vehicle wraps, wall murals, and more.

Common Applications

  • Retractable banners: Portable, professional, perfect for trade shows and lobbies
  • Vinyl banners: Indoor and outdoor, grommeted for hanging, extremely durable
  • Posters: Photo-quality prints for retail, events, and office décor
  • Foam board signs: Lightweight, rigid, ideal for presentations and point-of-purchase displays
  • Window graphics: Storefront branding, privacy film, promotional displays
  • Wall murals: Transform interior spaces with branded or decorative graphics
  • Trade show displays: Pop-ups, backdrops, table covers, and complete booth graphics

File Preparation Tips

  • Resolution: 150 DPI at full size for viewing distances over 3 feet. 300 DPI for close-up viewing
  • Color mode: CMYK for most large format work. RGB files will be converted
  • Bleed: Include at least 0.5" bleed on all sides
  • File format: PDF, AI, or high-resolution TIFF preferred
  • Fonts: Outline all fonts or embed them in your PDF
