Planning Your Training Manual

A well-produced training manual enhances learning, improves retention, and reflects professionalism. Whether it's employee onboarding, safety training, or professional development, the production quality matters.

Content Structure

  • Table of contents: Essential for navigation in manuals over 20 pages
  • Chapter/section dividers: Tabbed dividers for quick reference
  • Consistent formatting: Use the same heading styles, fonts, and layouts throughout
  • Visual aids: Diagrams, charts, photos, and infographics improve comprehension
  • Write-in spaces: For workbook-style manuals, include note areas and exercise sections
  • Appendices: Reference sheets, glossaries, and supplemental materials

Choosing the Right Binding

  • Coil binding: Best all-around choice — lays flat, folds back, durable. Ideal for reference manuals
  • Three-ring binder: Easy to update pages. Best for materials that change frequently
  • Perfect binding: Book-quality look. Best for permanent, polished manuals
  • Saddle-stitch: Cost-effective for thin manuals (under 64 pages)

Production Tips

  • Print a test copy before the full run
  • Consider color covers with B&W interior to balance cost and appearance
  • Use heavier cover stock (80-100lb) for durability
  • Include page numbers and section headers on every page
  • Order 10-15% extra for future needs
