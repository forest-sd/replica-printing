Why Direct Mail Still Works

Higher response rates than email, better ROI than you'd think.

Direct Mail by the Numbers

In an age of overflowing inboxes, direct mail cuts through the noise:

  • Direct mail response rates: 2.7-4.4% (vs. 0.6% for email)
  • 90% of direct mail gets opened (vs. 20-30% for email)
  • 70% of consumers say direct mail feels more personal than digital
  • Direct mail ROI: 29% (comparable to social media at 30%)

Best Practices

  • Target your list: The list matters more than the design. Targeted lists outperform general lists 3-5x
  • Clear call to action: One CTA per piece. Make it obvious what you want recipients to do
  • Track everything: Use unique URLs, QR codes, promo codes, or dedicated phone numbers
  • Test and iterate: A/B test headlines, offers, and formats. Small changes can drive big improvements
  • Consistency: Multiple touches outperform single mailings. Plan a series, not a one-off
  • Quality printing matters: A cheap-looking mailer gets tossed. Professional printing gets read

Formats That Work

  • Oversized postcards (6x9 or 6x11): No envelope to open, immediate visibility, cost-effective
  • Letters with personalization: Higher response rates for high-value offers
  • Self-mailers: Folded pieces that mail without an envelope
  • EDDM (Every Door Direct Mail): Reach every address on a route — no list needed
