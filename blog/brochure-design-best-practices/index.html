The Anatomy of an Effective Brochure

A well-designed brochure is a powerful sales tool — portable, tangible, and packed with information in an easily digestible format. Here's how to make yours work harder.

Fold Options

  • Tri-fold (letter fold): The most common. Six panels, compact, fits a #10 envelope
  • Z-fold: Unfolds like an accordion. Great for sequential information or maps
  • Gate fold: Dramatic reveal effect — panels fold inward to a center spread
  • Half-fold: Simple four-panel layout, larger panel sizes for impactful imagery
  • Roll fold: Panels fold inward sequentially. Good for step-by-step content

Design Tips

  • Start with the cover: Your front panel has one job — get someone to open the brochure. Strong headline, compelling image, clear value proposition
  • Organize by panel: Each panel should have a clear purpose. Don't spread one idea across multiple panels
  • Use hierarchy: Headlines → subheads → body text → call to action. Guide the reader's eye
  • Include a clear CTA: Phone number, website, QR code — make it easy to take the next step
  • Don't overcrowd: White space makes content more readable and the design more professional
